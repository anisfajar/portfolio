Have a question? Check out the FAQ

Curious about the roles, processes, or technology stack our team uses? Find the answers to the most common questions regarding our Software Developer team below.

What are Software Developer Services?

Software Developer Services (or Software Development Services) are professional offerings that cover the entire lifecycle of custom software—from planning, design, coding, testing, to implementation and maintenance—tailored to your specific business requirements.

What is the difference between custom software and off-the-shelf software?

Off-the-shelf software is a general solution sold commercially (e.g., Microsoft Office). Custom software is an application built from scratch specifically to meet your unique workflows and business challenges, giving you full control over features and scalability.

How does your software development process work?

We typically follow this cycle: 1. Discovery & Planning (gathering requirements), 2. Design (UX/UI and Architecture), 3. Development (coding and testing), 4. Deployment (launch), and 5. Maintenance & Support (post-launch upkeep).

How long does it take to build an application?

The duration varies significantly based on complexity, feature count, and data availability. A small project might take 2–3 months, while complex enterprise systems can take 6 months or more. We provide an accurate estimate after the initial Discovery phase.

Are there any hidden costs besides the development fee?

No. We are committed to transparency. Additional costs may only arise due to scope changes (Scope Creep) after the contract is signed, which we will communicate and agree upon beforehand through a formal Change Request.

Who owns the source code of the developed software?

Under our standard contract, you (the client) retain full rights and ownership of all source code upon final payment and project handover.
